ENDOCRINE DISRUPTORS
    Food & Drink
        Plastic packaging & containers — BPA and phthalates leach into food, especially when heated (microwaving plastic, hot liquids in plastic cups)
        Canned foods — BPA is commonly used in can linings
        Pesticide residues — conventionally grown produce often carries organophosphates and other agrochemicals
        Fatty fish — can accumulate PCBs and dioxins from contaminated water
        Tap water — may contain atrazine (herbicide), chlorine byproducts, pharmaceutical residues, and PFAS ("forever chemicals")
        Soy products — contain phytoestrogens (plant-based estrogen mimics); debated in terms of impact

    Personal Care & Cosmetics
        Deodorant / antiperspirant — aluminum compounds, parabens, triclosan (antibacterial)
        Cologne / perfume — synthetic musks, phthalates (used to make scent last longer, often hidden under "fragrance" on labels)
        Shampoo & conditioner — parabens, DEA/MEA compounds, synthetic fragrances
        Lotion & moisturizers — parabens, phthalates, oxybenzone (in some sunscreens)
        Nail polish — DBP (dibutyl phthalate), formaldehyde

    Household Products
        Soap (especially antibacterial) — triclosan, synthetic fragrances
        Cleaning products — alkylphenols, synthetic fragrances
        Non-stick cookware — PFAS (Teflon-related compounds) released at high heat
        Flame retardants — in furniture foam, mattresses, electronics
        Air fresheners — phthalates, synthetic musks

    Other Sources
        Receipts (thermal paper) — BPA absorbed directly through skin
        Dust — accumulates flame retardants and other chemicals from household items
        Pesticides — in homes, gardens, and agriculture
